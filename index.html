Investigating Stress-Induced Driving Behaviors: A Psychophysiological Analysis using Wearable Sensors

Wearable devices are essential for gathering psychophysiological data, especially in stress-induced driving scenarios. Our study, using the Empatica E4 device, focuses on wristband sensors to understand how specific driving conditions induce stress. Stress while driving can lead to negative emotions and unsafe behaviors, contributing to accidents. The research correlates vulnerability factors to stress, utilizing a validated questionnaire and assessing participants' performance in a driving simulator and virtual reality. Ten subjects participated in three experimental conditions: Low-Risk Driving, Medium-Risk Driving, and High-Risk Driving, providing insights into the relationship between driving conditions and stress.
